📋 Draft Recap

The Available Golfers list retired when pick 56 landed. In its place: one analyst's completely objective, legally-non-binding team grades.

1.GaryA−
The chalk doesn't apologize.

Drew the #1 seat and did the boring, correct thing: took Scottie Scheffler, the best golfer alive and the defending Open champion, and let everyone else get creative. Hatton is a top-ten machine who plays links golf angry, which is the correct way to play links golf. Koepka at +6000 in a major is either a bargain or a séance. Then he closed with Rickie Fowler, a pick made entirely by the heart. The heart remembers 2014. The scorecard may not.

💎 Steal: Hatton R2🚩 Sentimental: Fowler R7
2.RamA−
Drafted the entire Birkdale gift shop.

Tommy Fleetwood grew up in Southport — Royal Birkdale is functionally his backyard, and the crowd will treat him like a golden retriever who learned to putt. Lowry has a Claret Jug, Cam Smith has a Claret Jug, and MacIntyre is a Scot who thinks 40 mph wind is "a nice day out." A roster built by someone who understands what tournament this is. The only question is whether Cam Smith remembers he's Cam Smith.

💎 Steal: Lowry R3📍 Local knowledge: Fleetwood R1
3.RonnieB+
One pick doing a LOT of heavy lifting.

Morikawa in round 1 is a former Open champion with the best iron play on earth — sturdy. But the headline is Jordan Spieth in round 4: the man won the 2017 Open at this exact golf course, including the greatest bogey in major history off a driving range. If ghosts are real, Ronnie owns one. The supporting cast (Hovland, Burns) treats seaside golf like a personal insult, so this is three guys and a prayer. Good news: you only count four.

💎 Steal: Spieth R4 — won HERE in 2017🚩 Links allergy: Burns R3
4.GFB+
Main-character energy, questionable supporting cast.

Rory at #2 overall is the easiest pick anyone made all night, and Gotterup in round 3 is the sneaky-sicko selection — the man has decided he only plays well on this side of the Atlantic. Bryson on a links course remains science's greatest experiment: 190 ball speed into a 30 mph crosswind, results may vary. And then there's Patrick Reed, who will grind out pars from a parking lot and make the whole group uncomfortable doing it. Chaotic. Watchable. Possibly good?

💎 Steal: Gotterup R3🚩 Science experiment: Bryson R2
5.AceB
Two aircraft carriers and a jet ski.

Rahm and Xander back-to-back is as good a 1-2 punch as this draft allowed — a two-time major champ and the guy who lifted the Jug in '24. Then, with the entire adult population of the leaderboard available, Ace took Akshay Bhatia at +10000 in round 3 — a reach so bold Vegas briefly halted betting to ask if he knew something. Henley is quietly the most underrated pick nobody will talk about. Top-heavy, but in a top-4 format, heavy tops win.

💎 Steal: Henley R4🚩 Reach: Bhatia R3 at +10000
6.PhillipsB−
Passed on the odds board to draft a memory.

Justin Rose at pick 5 raised eyebrows — until you remember a 17-year-old Rose holed out from the rough on Birkdale's 72nd hole in '98 and made a whole country cry. A reach with a soundtrack. Åberg in round 2 is real value, JT brings two majors and one mood swing, and Brian Harman in round 6 is the heist of the draft: won the 2023 Open by six while everyone complained about him. No ceiling-raiser, but a shockingly firm floor. Like Harman himself.

💎 Steal: Harman R6 — '23 Champion Golfer🚩 Reach: Rose R1
7.DragonC+
A beautiful spreadsheet with a links problem.

Matt Fitzpatrick in round 1 is tidy, sensible, very U.S. Open of him. But Wyndham Clark's career record at the Open reads like a ransom note, and Cantlay at links golf is the human embodiment of a 45-minute pre-shot routine into a double bogey. Min Woo Lee is the redemption arc — chef hat on, let him cook, the kid was built for weather. On paper this team is fine. Unfortunately the Open is not played on paper; it's played in a wind tunnel with pot bunkers.

💎 Steal: Min Woo R4🚩 Links allergy: Clark R3, Cantlay R5
